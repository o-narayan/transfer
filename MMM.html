Merced requires a GPA of 2.0 in all the highlighted courses separately.

Riverside allows the biology/chemistry/physics sequence to substitute for MATH 10AB and MATH 46 for transfer admission.

Santa Barbara allows linear algebra or ordinary differential equations to substitute for multivariable calculus for transfer admission, but all of these are recommended.

The underlined columns are the UC Transfer Pathway for Mathematics


Red: required for admission eligibility, and for a transfer admission guarantee (TAG) where TAGs are available

Orange: recommended prior to transfer to improve time to degree and (for non-TAG admission) chances for admission

Green: required for the major, but can be completed after transfer


  • Completion of the pathway courses with suitable grades (see table above), and campus-specific additional requirements, will make a student eligible for admission to all UC campuses and eligible to apply for TAG to any one of several campuses. Some campuses require much less than the full pathway for TAG admission.
  • Completion of the Mathematics Associate Degree for Transfer (ADT) with suitable grades (see table above), if the courses are articulated by the UC campuses, and campus-specific additional requirements are fulfilled, fulfills the requirements for a TAG at UC Irvine and, depending on whether a course on linear algebra or ODEs is completed, at Santa Cruz or Riverside.
  • Course numbers that are shown are the course numbers at each campus. Please consult ASSIST to find the courses articulated at your community college to these requirements. In general, all the courses in a column are articulated to the same courses at a community college, but this is not always true. If you are interested in several campuses, check ASSIST for the articulations for each of them.
